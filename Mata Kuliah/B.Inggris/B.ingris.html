COURSE IDENTITY

Subject : ENGLISH
Subject Code : VSI1A4
Credits : 1
Semester : 1
Academic Year : 2024/2025

Course Description
In this course, you will be learning and developing your English speaking, listening, reading, and writing skills, with an emphasis on leveraging your speaking skills. You are also learning cross cultural understanding (CCU) and intercultural communication as the content of your language learning. In other words, you will be learning about CCU and intercultural communication as you are practicing your English skills. As your English skills are getting better and better, it is expected that you will be able to use it to learn CCU deeper. In order to meet that goal, the resources include various topics regarding one’s profession, cross culture understanding, and intercultural communication delivered in the forms of popular essays, pictogram, short movies, and vlogs. Through your conscientious and dedicated work through the whole course, you will find yourself well prepared to participate in conversations about yourself, your own and others’ culture, your dream job, and your dream country.

COURSE LEARNING OBJECTIVES
Upon the completion of the course, you will be able to:
1. use simple sentences in present tense to present some interesting facts about themselves in the form of a monolog with “intermediate mid” fluency level.
2. use simple and compound sentences in present to participate in short conversations about themselves, especially about their experience of being freshmen with “intermediate mid” fluency level.
3. use complex sentences in present tense to describe your dream job with sufficient details regarding the name of a position as well as the task and responsibilities pertaining to the position with “intermediate mid” fluency level.
4. use simple, compound, and complex sentences in present tense to describe (1) the work culture in their home country; (2) the work culture in their dream country; (3) similarities and differences of two countries; and (4) your dream job abroad with the fluency level of ­intermediate-high.
